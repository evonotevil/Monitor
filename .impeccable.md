## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary audience**: Lilith Games compliance team (legal BPs) and department leadership
- **Context**: BPs check on mobile throughout the week for updates; leadership reviews on desktop during meetings or at their desk. Both contexts are equally important.
- **Job to be done**: Quickly scan global gaming regulatory changes, assess risk priority, and track follow-up tasks — without needing to read every source article

### Brand Personality
- **Voice**: Warm, approachable, informative — like a well-designed internal newsletter
- **Tone**: Professional but not intimidating; structured but not sterile
- **3 words**: Clear, Trustworthy, Efficient
- **Emotional goals**: Confidence that nothing important is missed; calm in the face of complex global regulatory landscape

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Current direction**: Light theme with dark header bar, color-coded accent system (7 category colors), monospace details for metadata, card-based layout. This direction is confirmed as good — refine, don't reinvent.
- **Typography**: Inter (variable) for body, JetBrains Mono for metadata/labels. PingFang SC / Noto Sans SC as CJK fallback.
- **Color system**: Warm off-white background (#F9F9F7), 7 accent colors for categories (red/green/purple/magenta/teal/blue/orange), dark header (#1A1A1A)
- **Anti-references**: Dense legal documents, overly playful consumer apps, generic dashboard templates
- **Theme**: Light mode only (report is often printed/PDF'd)

### Design Principles
1. **Scan-first hierarchy**: The most important information (risk level, region, status) must be visible without reading full text. Support rapid triage.
2. **Mobile-desktop parity**: Neither platform is secondary. The report must be equally effective on a 375px phone screen and a 1400px desktop monitor.
3. **Quiet confidence**: Use color and typography to create clarity, not drama. Accent colors signal category — not decoration.
4. **Content density over chrome**: Minimize decorative elements. Every pixel should serve information delivery or navigation.
5. **Three-zone integrity**: The archived/news/active structure is the backbone of the report. Visual design must reinforce these three zones clearly.
